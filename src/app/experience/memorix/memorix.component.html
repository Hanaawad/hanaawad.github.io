PROBLEM STATEMENT

University students worldwide struggle with memorizing new information. 70% of what they learn is forgotten within the first 24 hours. Students try to counter this with inefficient techniques.

PROJECT GOALS

  • Redesign of current Memorix website to make it simpler for students
  • Provide to students empower students digital tool to create personalized and efficient memory journeys that can be shared among their academic community and improve learning capabilities
  • Improve learning capabilities for students
  • Improve the user experience of memory technique
  • Introduce mobile and tablet optimisation

DEFINING THE FLOW

    One of the first steps was to look at the current way the app was structured to make sure there aren't any problems in the flow. Since this was an existing app there wasn't too much room for change but we did decide to add an onboarding flow and tidy app simor minor interactions.

RESEARCH METHODS

  • Customer interviews
  • Prototype testing
  • Persona
  • Customer journey

GATHERING INSIGHTS

    After collecting the recordings from the user interviews, I conducted affinity mapping with my teammate to synthesise the pains identified. We grouped these problems under common themes and features in the platform.s.

SKETCHING

    Sketching before wireframing allowed to try out a multitude of ideas and iterate them before settling on one and to come up with the best solution.

WIREFRAMING

    After defining the flow the next step is to focus on space allocation and prioritization of content, functionalities available and experience in more detail on a screen-by-screen level. The main focus was the Scheduler page planning which is the heart of the app.
